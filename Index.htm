About True Worshippers

Founded in 2020 by Apostle Solomon S. Adebowale and Reverend Mrs. Debby Adebowale, True Worshippers Evangelical Ministries International is a spiritual family dedicated to raising authentic worshipers who encounter God in spirit and truth.

We are called to equip believers for the work of ministry, build up the body of Christ, and carry the message of reconciliation to the ends of the earth. Through sound teaching, daily devotionals, and worship, we foster an atmosphere where lives are transformed and identity in Christ is established.

RELEASE YOURSELF FROM BITTERNESS 1

Apostle Segun D. A. & Reverend Mrs. Debby F. A. Solomon
Hymn: “What a Friend We Have in Jesus”
"Bitterness is a poison you drink hoping others will die. Release it, and let God heal your soul."

Ephesians 4:31-32 & Hebrews 12:15 (NKJV)

Ephesians 4:31-32: “Let all bitterness, wrath, anger, clamor, and evil speaking be put away from you, with all malice. And be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, even as God in Christ forgave you.”

Hebrews 12:15: “Looking carefully lest anyone fall short of the grace of God; lest any root of bitterness springing up cause trouble, and by this many become defiled.”

Devotional Message

Bitterness is one of the most destructive forces in the human heart. It often begins as a small seed—an offense, a disappointment, an injustice—but if left unchecked, it grows into a root that defiles many (Hebrews 12:15). The apostle Paul warns us to put away all bitterness, wrath, anger, and malice. These are not merely suggestions; they are commands for our spiritual health.

Why must we release bitterness? Because it imprisons us, not the one who wronged us. Job understood this when he said, “If I have rejoiced at the destruction of him who hated me, or lifted myself up when evil found him... (indeed I have not allowed my mouth to sin)” (Job 31:29-30). David also modeled this heart: when Shimei cursed him, David refused to retaliate, saying, “Let him alone, and let him curse; for so the Lord has ordered him” (2 Samuel 16:11-12).

The ultimate example is our Lord Jesus. On the cross, He prayed, “Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they do” (Luke 23:34). Stephen, the first martyr, echoed this: “Lord, do not charge them with this sin” (Acts 7:60). If Jesus could forgive His murderers, can we not forgive those who have offended us? Paul reminds us that we are to forgive “as Christ forgave you” (Colossians 3:13).

Holding onto bitterness also hinders our prayers. Jesus taught: “And whenever you stand praying, if you have anything against anyone, forgive him, that your Father in heaven may also forgive your trespasses” (Mark 11:25). Unforgiveness blocks the flow of God's grace in our lives. It is like drinking poison and expecting the other person to die.

Today, ask the Holy Spirit to search your heart. Is there anyone you have not forgiven? Any root of bitterness? Release them to God. He is the righteous Judge. As Paul wrote, “Beloved, do not avenge yourselves, but rather give place to wrath; for it is written, ‘Vengeance is Mine, I will repay,’ says the Lord” (Romans 12:19). Let go, and let God heal your heart.

Prophetic Prayer Points

Father, I confess any bitterness hidden in my heart; I bring it to Your light.
Lord, help me to forgive those who have hurt me, just as You have forgiven me.
I release every person who has offended me into Your hands; I let go of the right to revenge.
Father, uproot every root of bitterness in my life by the power of Your Spirit.
I renounce anger, wrath, and malice; I put on kindness, tenderheartedness, and forgiveness.
Lord, heal the wounds that gave birth to bitterness; pour Your balm of Gilead into my soul.
I declare that I will not allow any root of bitterness to defile me or those around me.
Father, give me the grace to pray for my enemies and bless those who curse me.
I receive the peace of God that passes all understanding, guarding my heart and mind.
Thank You, Lord, for setting me free from the prison of bitterness. I walk in freedom today.

Word of Encouragement

Beloved, forgiveness is not excusing the offense; it is releasing the offender into God's just and merciful hands. You may feel that the wound is too deep, but God's grace is deeper. He specializes in healing broken hearts. Take a step today—speak forgiveness, even if the feelings aren't there yet. Feelings follow choices. Choose freedom, and He will restore your soul.

“Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, even as God in Christ forgave you.” — Ephesians 4:32
