Union with Christ

Daily Devotional by Apostle Daniel A. Solomon
"God does not despise your exhaustion; He meets you in it. Your weariness is not a sign of failure, but an invitation to draw strength from His unending grace."
"Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest."
— Matthew 11:28 (NIV)

It is a spiritual reality: the journey of faith is not without moments of deep weariness and frustration. You may feel drained—not just physically, but emotionally and spiritually. Perhaps you have been praying without seeing answers, sowing without reaping, or striving without breaking through. In these moments, it is easy to believe God is far away, or that your labor is in vain. But Scripture reminds us that even the young grow tired and stumble, yet those who wait upon the Lord will renew their strength.

God is not surprised by your fatigue. He understands the weight you carry. The invitation of Christ is not to "try harder," but to "come closer." Your weariness can become a sacred space—a place where self-reliance ends and divine reliance begins. Do not mistake this season for abandonment; it is often God's way of redirecting your dependence from your own strength to His sovereign power.

If frustration has clouded your vision, take a moment to pause and reposition your heart. Release the timelines, surrender the outcomes, and lay down the burden you were never meant to carry alone. God is not only the God of sudden breakthroughs but also the God of daily bread—the One who gives strength for this moment, grace for this hour, and hope for this day.

Today's Prayer

  1. Thank You, Lord, for Your understanding and compassion when I am tired and overwhelmed.
  2. Forgive me for the times I have relied on my own strength and resented Your timing.
  3. I bring my frustrations and weariness to Your feet—I lay them down before You now.
  4. Renew my strength according to Your promise; help me to wait upon You with hope.
  5. Restore joy where duty has drained me, and peace where worry has wearied me.
  6. Teach me to recognize Your gentle leading when I am too tired to see clearly.
  7. Provide divine rest—spirit, soul, and body—as only You can give.
  8. Guard my heart from comparison and bitterness when my journey feels slower than others'.
  9. Let me find comfort in Your presence more than in a changed circumstance.
  10. Thank You for being my rest and my refuge, today and always.

Remember

Even in weariness, you are moving forward. God is not only with you in the struggle—He is strengthening you through it.

Apostle Daniel A. Solomon
